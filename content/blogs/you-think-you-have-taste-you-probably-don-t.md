## Taste Is Not What You Think It Is

Most people think taste is about having good opinions. Liking the right music, wearing the right things, using the right apps. But that is not taste. That is preference. Taste is something harder and rarer.

### Strong Opinions About Small Things

One sign you are developing taste is that you start caring about things that seem too small to care about. The weight of a button tap. The way an error message is phrased. The exact amount of white space between two elements on a screen.

This is not **fussiness**. This is taste doing its job. When something feels slightly off, that discomfort is information. It means your eye has gotten ahead of your hand. You can feel the problem before you can fully explain it.

#### The Spotify Restraint

Spotify knows this. Their Discover Weekly feature works partly because it respects your attention. It does not overwhelm you. It makes one confident bet each week and trusts you to follow it. 

That confidence, that restraint, is a taste decision. Someone at Spotify had to say no to more and yes to less. That takes courage, because users often ask for more. Taste means committing to a vision even when the easier path is to just give people what they ask for.
